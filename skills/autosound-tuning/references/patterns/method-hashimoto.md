# The Hashimoto method — by-ear stage building (an alternative/complementary approach)

> 🧩 **PATTERN REPOSITORY — starting hypotheses, not rules.** Everything here is "in similar conditions this often worked": a **starting point**, validated by measurement/ear, never a mandate. See [`knowledge-architecture.md`](file:///skills/autosound-tuning/references/core/knowledge-architecture.md).

A complete **by-ear** method from the Japanese installer **Hashimoto** (translated by Anton Nikolaev, a video summary). The goal = **building the soundstage**. It's an ALTERNATIVE/complement to our REW measurement process (`process-phases.md`) — useful where we work by ear (Phase 5) or without full REW. The signature technique: **"first the optimal filter SLOPE, then the optimal FREQUENCY"**, each joint separately, each side separately.

## Order: crossovers (slope→freq) → polarity per band → delays (verticals→center) → EQ (last) → voicing on music
0. **Break in the components** (new ones — break them in like an engine; otherwise during alignment the midbass/mid/sub "fall apart" one by one). A rough start-tune → break-in → precise.
1. **Initial values** (to get it "playing"): processor default; channel routing. Starting crossovers: **sub↔midbass 60–80 · midbass↔mid 200–1000** (a large cone mid — lower, a small/dome one — up to 1000) · **mid↔tweeter 3–6k** (2-way: midbass↔tweeter 3.5–6k).
2. **Initial delays:** measure from each driver's center **to the tip of the NOSE** (head on the headrest → that point between the ears). Ref = the farthest driver (the sub); the rest = a delta.

## Polarity — BY EAR, per band ⚠️ A VARIANT
A mono vocal. Leave only the **midbasses** → the image should focus ~center. If it falls apart / sticks to the drivers — **flip the polarity of BOTH** (sometimes just ONE; or check the wiring). Then the same for the **mid**, the **tweeters — last** (there λ ≈ the distance between the ears → phase is hard to determine, come back later). Image shifted → a little with delays (to the left → delay the right one).
> ⚠️ **CONTRADICTS Wehmeyer/our §16** ("don't set polarity by the pair's center image — it leads to 180° anti-phase after delays"). We keep it as a **variant** (Hashimoto does it EARLY for a rough start, then refines the tweeter phase by fusion after choosing the slope). Check by summation (`diagnostic §9`); on conflict — trust the summation.

## Filters: SLOPE → FREQUENCY, each side separately
**Why L/R separately and the parameters can DIFFER:** filters rotate the phase, the drivers are at different angles → you hear them differently. **A big mistake — making the crossover frequencies/orders the same L/R.** The guide is the EAR, not the numbers. *(Consistent with our crossover-asymmetry §15.)*
- **SUB (first):** LPF off, full band. Subsonic HPF ~20 Hz — change the ORDER, listen to a double bass 80–100 (crispest/fast/energetic). Then LPF 80–100 — change the slope (bass juicy/deep). Then the FREQUENCIES (same logic). Recommendation: subsonic 20 Hz (higher — for ported); LPF not too low, slope **12 or 24 dB/oct** (6 dB → overlap/boom; 48 dB → phase distortion).
- **MIDBASS (2 tracks: mid/treble + mid/low):** HPF — low 25–32, track 1 (mid/treble), slope → the vocal is rich, you hear nuances + reverberation. LPF ~10–12k, track 2 (mid/low), slope → the bass is crisp/springy. Then the frequencies: for the HPF listen to the treble/mid, for the LPF — the bass. HPF **at the driver's resonance or above** (a driver plays better away from its resonance; not below 40–50). LPF — preliminary (final at the joint with the tweeter). Then the **sub↔midbass levels** (a smooth transition): adjust the sub's LPF frequency + the sub's LEVEL; don't touch the midbass LPF (the midbass = the foundation).
- **3-way — the mid↔midbass joint:** the split frequency **≥ an octave above the mid's resonance**, but no higher than the midbass's pistonic→modal-regime limit. Find the limit: track 2, LPF 2nd order, lower the frequency until the sound is maximally energetic/dense/articulate. (Here: frequency → slope → frequency again.)
- **MID** (a track with few instruments, full band — Jennifer Warnes): HPF = the midbass LPF, slope (listen to the low end + the upper overtones); LPF slope (drums — springy, with attack); at the midbass↔mid joint — the **vocal is natural/clean**. LPF frequency preliminary. The mid↔midbass levels → they sound **fused, like one driver**.
- **TWEETER** (a track with metallic sounds/cymbals; midbass+mid on, sub off): HPF = the mid/midbass LPF, but **necessarily above the tweeter's resonance** (otherwise harsh/dirty); a higher HPF = lighter/cleaner (especially 6–12 dB). Slope → cymbals lively/natural, long smooth decays, not metallic/glassy (overtones affect the fundamentals!).
  - **Tweeter polarity — NOW** (after choosing the slope!): normal vs inverted — the tweeter must **FUSE** with the midbass/mid, not sound on its own. Where it fused = the right phase.
  - Tweeter frequency: leave only the tweeter — cymbal hits with long decays, air, without excess ringing/hiss. Then + midbass/mid: as the tweeter blends in → set the **LEVEL** where the vocal is most natural. For frequency correction — better to adjust the **MIDBASS filter, not the tweeter**.
  - ⚠️ Changing one band's level affects the joint both up and down the chain (a cascade).

## Front delays (Time Correction) — the heart of the stage
The goal = focused, localized images (as the engineer intended). A mono recording. **"Verticals"** = align midbass↔mid↔tweeter of each side SEPARATELY. Sub off, passenger side: (2-way) the tweeter↔midbass joint — note the tweeter's starting delay, add small increments, watch the image → it localizes near the tweeter as ONE source (usually a small correction; if large increments change nothing — install/orientation/enclosure errors). (3-way in 2 steps: midbass↔mid, then →tweeter.) Image on the dash → good, then the driver side (harder: the panel + the wheel). **Full front:** mono exactly center from one point; if a band smears — nudge the drivers' delays (driver side) 1–2 increments. **Sub:** a double-bass track, move the sub's delay → crispest/dynamic/with drive, without boom.

## EQ — the LAST step
After crossovers+delays (EQ also rotates phase). Mono sines / narrow noises of different frequencies → must come from the CENTER. Play quietly, low→high. In the bass there's no localization (λ > the cabin); closer to the top the image gathers to the center. Image shifted right at a frequency → the right is louder there → **take a little off the right + add to the left** → center (to the left → vice versa). Go through the whole spectrum. ⚠️ **At 2.5–5 kHz the sound seems louder/shouts — do NOT touch EQ there** (hearing sensitivity); only determine the relative L/R loudness. A serious shift at a frequency → go back to the earlier steps. An excess/dip at a frequency = resonances/install → ideally treat the cause.

## Voicing on real music (emotion/character) — = our Phase 6
Then tune the "academic" system on **favorite music** (live instruments are better — richer; the brain compares with a real instrument). An instrument's character = the fundamentals + the **overtones/harmonics** → affect both the fundamentals and the harmonics. Brighter overall: ↓~200 + ↑~2k, then balance the harmonics. ↑100/300/1k + harmonics = more energy. → by-ear details in `voicing-by-ear.md`.
> **Take breaks** — hearing fatigues (the untrained ear faster), you stop hearing the effect of changes.

## What to take into our process
- The technique **"slope→frequency, each side separately"** — powerful for by-ear filter choice (Phase 5 / when REW is unavailable). · Joint frequencies/orders can differ L/R = a **by-ear VARIANT** (the default — L/R symmetry: the same type+order+frequency for a stable phantom center; asymmetry only when symmetry won't image → verify imaging; `filter-types §L/R symmetry`, `diagnostic §15`). · EQ centering with mono sines + **don't touch 2.5–5k**. · Break in components + breaks. · Polarity per band = a **variant** (check by summation).
