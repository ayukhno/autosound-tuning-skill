## 2. What Worked (Successful Decisions & Techniques)
* **Symmetrical LR4 Crossovers:** Synergistic crossover transitions using симетричні зрізи LR4 (60 Hz / 300 Hz / 3500 Hz) provided a solid acoustic phase baseline.
* **VCP EQ Layering (Crucial Insight):** Placing the main СЧ corrections (`453 Hz` Left and `459 Hz` Right) on the **Virtual Channel Processing (VCP) EQ layer** instead of output channels saved the acoustic phase summation at the 300 Hz crossover point, preventing major dips.
* **Subwoofer Overlap (60 Hz LPF):** Setting subwoofer LPF to `60 Hz LR4` (overlapping with woofers HPF 60 Hz LR4) successfully compensated for sedan cabin cancellation at 50-60 Hz, giving +3.1 dB summation and a solid front-bass illusion.
* **Presence Recess (2.1 - 3.4 kHz):** Shaping a smooth -2.0 dB "presence dip" on midranges based on SQ-Comp-Ref targets successfully pushed the singer deep behind the windshield and focused the mono-center.
* **Symmetrical +1.0 dB Gain Elevate:** Compensating for the overall energy loss of Presence Recess by raising both СЧ and ВЧ gains symmetrically by `+1.0 dB` successfully lifted the stage back to eye-level while preserving depth.

## 4. New Techniques & Generalizable Know-how
* `📚 [VCP EQ over Output EQ]:` At crossover junctions, use Virtual Channel EQ for narrow high-Q cuts to preserve phase coherence of the individual drivers.
* `📚 [Vertical Stage Elevate]:` A deep Presence Recess (-2 to -4 dB on СЧ) reduces perceived soundstage height. Symmetrically boosting СЧ and ВЧ levels by +1.0 dB restores height without ruining depth.
* `📚 [Physical Baseline Priority]:` Do not use APF on door woofers to correct local cabin anomalies; physical phase symmetry and simple Notch filters are subjectively more stable than electrical phase shifts.

## 5. DSP & Hardware Quirks (Helix DSP Ultra S)
* **Virtual EQ Summation:** Virtual Front Left/Right EQ filters are electrically added to the individual output filters. Total electrical attenuation must be simulated by summing both layers.
* **Sample Rate dependency:** All digital sample calculations for delays MUST assume 96 kHz internal processing for Helix DSP Ultra S. Physical milliseconds (ms) remain the universal source of truth.

## 6. Vehicle Cabin Anomalies (Passat B8 Sedan)
* **150-160 Hz Left Woofer Dip:** Severe -11..13 dB cancellation on the left door woofer due to cabin geometry. Unfixable by EQ; any boost causes coil distortion.
* **70-100 Hz Right Woofer Peak:** Severe cabin resonance, successfully resolved via a deep notch: `73.0 Hz | -9.0 dB | Q = 3.5`.
* **190 Hz Cabin Boom:** A major natural cabin resonance around 188-193 Hz on both sides. Easily controlled by narrow notches: `188.0 Hz | -5.5 dB | Q=5.0` on Left, `193.0 Hz | -3.0 dB | Q=6.0` on Right.
